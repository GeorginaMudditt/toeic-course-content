Answers

Exercise 1 - Speaking (Questions 1-8)

  1. What do you do? > Present Simple (habit, routine, things that are permanent).
    Examples: I am an English teacher. I teach adults and children.
  2. How would you describe your role to someone who has no knowledge of the industry you work in? > Present Simple (habit, routine, things that are permanent).
    Examples: I teach English to students from different backgrounds. They do not speak in their native language in class.
  3. Describe your typical workday. > Present Simple (habit, routine, things that are permanent) / Present Continuous (temporary projects, things happening at the moment).
    Examples: I mostly work from home. I start work at 8am. At the moment, I am working towards Qualiopi certification, so I am writing lots of policy documents and dealing with lots of administration.
  4. Did you go to university? If so, what did you study? > Past Simple (in the past, finished).
    Examples: I went to Edith Cowan University in Perth. I graduated in 2016.
  5. What was your first job when you finished your studies? > Past Simple (in the past, finished).
    Examples: I worked in a shop. I didn't like my first job.
  6. Have you ever worked in another country? > Present Perfect (life experience, until now).
    Examples: I have never worked in another country. I have worked as a teacher in Brazil and Japan.
  7. What has been your biggest professional achievement so far? > Present Perfect (life experience, until now).
    Examples: My biggest professional achievement has been leading a project that has increased our customer base by 30%. One of my biggest achievements has been developing a new marketing strategy that has improved our brand visibility.
  8. What has been the biggest challenge you have faced in your career so far? > Present Perfect (life experience, until now).
    Examples: The biggest challenge I have faced so far has been managing a team under tight deadlines. One of the biggest challenges I have faced has been adapting to rapid changes in a startup environment.
