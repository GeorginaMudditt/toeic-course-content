Passive Voice 2

Introduction

The passive voice is used when we want to focus on the action or the person/thing affected by the action, rather than who or what performs the action. It is also used in formal writing, academic contexts, and when you want to sound more objective or diplomatic. Consider the difference:

Direct: You have made several errors in your report.
Diplomatic: Several errors have been made in your report.

Grammar Focus: Advanced Passive Forms

Structure Active Passive Structure (passive)
Present Perfect Simple They have closed the shop. The shop has been closed. Subject + have/has been + past participle
Past Perfect Simple Someone had stolen the painting. The painting had been stolen. Subject + had been + past participle
Modals You must finish the work. The work must be finished. Subject + modal + be + past participle
