TOEICⓇ Speaking: Your Future Career

Speaking

Part 1: Read Aloud

In the TOEIC Speaking test, the first task asks you to read a short text aloud. You see the text on the screen and speak clearly, with good pronunciation and pacing.

Instructions:

Read the text aloud clearly and carefully.

Choosing a future career is an important decision, but it is not always easy. Many students feel pressure to choose the perfect job immediately. However, it is useful to take time to think about your interests, your personality, and your skills.

Some people prefer practical work, while others enjoy creative or scientific activities. It is also important to consider working conditions, salary, training opportunities, and work-life balance.

Talking to teachers, relatives, or professional advisers can help you discover different possibilities. You do not need to know your entire future today. Many adults change careers during their lives.

The most important thing is to stay curious, continue learning, and choose a path that motivates and challenges you.

Part 2: Describing Images

Practise the TOEIC-style “describe a picture” task. Look at each image carefully before you speak.

Instructions:

Look at the images. Describe what you can see in each picture. Compare the jobs and discuss which job you would prefer.

Part 3: Discussion Questions

Answer the questions in 2–3 sentences, giving examples where possible.

  1. What subjects are you most interested in at school? Why?

  2. What job would you like to do in the future?

  3. Would you like to work in another country one day? Why or why not?

  4. Do you think it is better to choose a job with a high salary or a job you really love?

  5. What jobs are popular with young people in your country?

  6. Who influences young people most when they choose a career?

Part 5: Opinion Task

Instructions: Give your opinion and explain your ideas.

Topic

What are the most important things to consider when choosing a career?

You can discuss:

  • salary
  • job stability
  • enjoyment
  • social status
  • work-life balance
  • opportunities for travel
  • helping other people
  • flexible working hours
  • career progression

Useful expressions

  • In my opinion…
  • I believe that…
  • One important factor is…
  • Personally, I would prefer…
  • On the other hand…
  • I agree/disagree because…
