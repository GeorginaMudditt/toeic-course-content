Both, Either, Neither

Explanation

Both ... and

We use both ... and to connect two positive ideas.

Meaning: A and B are true

Structure: both + noun/pronoun + and + noun/pronoun

  • She likes both coffee and tea.
  • They are both friendly and helpful.
  • I can speak both English and Spanish.

Either ... or

We use either ... or to talk about a choice between two options.

Meaning: A or B (one of them)

Structure: either + option 1 + or + option 2

  • You can have either pasta or rice.
  • We will go either today or tomorrow.
  • She is either tired or bored.

Neither ... nor

We use neither ... nor to connect two negative ideas.

Meaning: Not A and not B

Structure: neither + option 1 + nor + option 2

  • He likes neither coffee nor tea.
  • She is neither happy nor sad.
  • We have neither time nor money.

  4. She is happy. She is excited.
    → She is
  5. He doesn't eat meat. He doesn't eat fish.
    → He eats
