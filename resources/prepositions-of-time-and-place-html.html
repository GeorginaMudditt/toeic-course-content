Prepositions of Time and Place

What are prepositions?

Prepositions are little words that show:

  • time (when?)
  • place / position (where?)
  • movement (where to?)

Examples:

  • at 7 o'clock
  • on Monday
  • in France
  • under the table

Grammar Point #1: Prepositions of Time

AT

We use at for times and precise moments.

  • I usually wake up at 6 o'clock.
  • Is the meeting at midday?
  • I didn't work at the weekend.

You may also see "on the weekend" in US/AUS English. UK English usually says "at the weekend".

ON

We use on for days and dates.

  • They are going back to school on Monday.
  • Please call me on Friday morning.
  • He will arrive on 12th June.
  • I usually go to a restaurant on my birthday.
IN

We use in for months, years, seasons, and long periods.

  • I am going to Spain in July.
  • I moved to Paris in 2025.
  • We don't use the swimming pool in winter.
  • She always drinks coffee in the morning.

We say in the morning / afternoon / evening, but at night.

Les dates en anglais

  • UK: on utilise souvent les ordinaux (1st, 2nd, 3rd...) -> 13th June.
  • AUS: on ecrit souvent sans ordinaux -> 13 June.
  • US: mois avant le jour -> June 13.

Attention aux formats chiffres:

  • 13/06/26 = 13 juin 2026 (UK). En US: 06/13/26.
  • 05/08/22 peut etre ambigu (UK: 5 aout / US: 8 mai).

Pour eviter toute confusion, ecrivez le mois en lettres: 5 August 2022 / August 5, 2022.

Grammar Point #2: Prepositions of Place

IN

We use in when something is inside a space.

  • The camera is in the box.
ON

We use on when something touches a surface.

  • The pencil is on the table.
UNDER

We use under when something is below another thing.

  • The cat is under the chair.
NEXT TO

We use next to when two things are beside each other.

  • The plant is next to the desk.
BETWEEN

We use between for something in the middle of two things.

  • The bank is between the cafe and the hotel.

Grammar Point #4: Common movement prepositions

TO

Use to for movement and direction.

  • I go to school on the train.
  • He walks to the station with his brother.
  • I want to travel to India this summer.
FROM

Use from for the starting point.

  • They come from Italy.
  • She took a taxi from the station to the office.
INTO

Use into for movement from outside to inside.

  • We will go into the room when the meeting starts.
  • Francois dived into the swimming pool.
  • Go into the house and wash your hands, please.
