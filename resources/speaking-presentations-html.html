Language

Making a first impression

  • "I'd like to begin with a question ..."
  • "Let me start with a quick story ..."
  • "Let me give you some context first ..."
  • "Here's why this topic matters ..."

Describing visuals and data

  • "This chart illustrates ..."
  • "What stands out here is ..."
  • "As you can see from this graph ..."
  • "There's a clear trend towards ..."
  • "The most significant change is ..."

Highlighting key messages

  • "The key point here is ..."
  • "What's important to remember is ..."
  • "The main takeaway is ..."
  • "This is crucial because ..."
  • "This has a direct impact on ..."

Giving your opinion

  • "It appears that ..."
  • "From a business perspective ..."
  • "In my opinion/view ..."
  • "You could argue that ..."
  • "The evidence suggests that ..."
  • "It is likely that ..."

Handling questions

  • "That's a great question."
  • "Let me think about that for a moment."
  • "I'll come back to that at the end."
  • "What I can say is ..."
  • "Does that answer your question?"

Ending memorably

  • "To leave you with one idea ..."
  • "So, what does this mean for us?"
  • "The bottom line is ..."
  • "I'm happy to discuss this further."

Stress patterns

In English, we stress certain words in each sentence, which creates rhythm.

Read these sentences and identify the stressed words:

  1. "I'd like to begin today with a question about customer loyalty."
  2. "What stands out here is the rapid growth in online sales."
  3. "We can see a clear trend towards remote work."
  4. "The bottom line is that we need to reduce costs in as many ways as we can."

So which words do we stress?

  • We usually stress content words (main verbs, nouns, adjectives).
  • We do not usually stress functional words (auxiliary verbs, articles, and prepositions).
