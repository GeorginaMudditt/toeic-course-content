Theme 1: Grammar Basics

Grammar Focus #1: Past Simple (verb to be)

Form

Subject Positive Negative Questions
I / He / She / It was was not (wasn't) Was I/he/she/it...?
You / We / They were were not (weren't) Were you/we/they...?

Examples:

  • I was in Rome last week.
  • They weren't at the hotel.
  • Were you on holiday in July?

Grammar Focus #2: Question Words

Common Question Words

Word Use Example
Who ask about a person Who did you go with?
Where ask about a place Where did you stay?
When ask about time When did you travel?
What ask about things/general info What was the weather like?
How ask about a process or a feeling How did you travel?
How much / How many ask about price/quantity (How much = uncountable nouns, How many = countable nouns) How much was the ticket? / How many hours was the journey?
Why ask about reason Why did you choose that destination?
Which ask to choose Which place was your favourite?

Grammar Focus #3: Past Simple (regular verbs)

Form

Structure Rule Example
Positive Subject + verb + -ed I visited Paris.
Negative Subject + did not + base verb I didn't visit Paris.
Question Did + subject + base verb Did you visit Paris?

Important: -ed ONLY in positive sentences.

Spelling Rules

  • Most verbs: + -ed -> work -> worked
  • Verbs ending in -e: + -d -> live -> lived
  • Consonant + y -> -ied -> study -> studied
  • Consonant-vowel-consonant -> double final consonant -> stop -> stopped

Note: These are regular verbs. There is also a long list of irregular verbs that do not use -ed endings (e.g. go -> went, see -> saw). We will look at these later.

Important Note: Prepositions

Look at these phrases from the previous exercise:

  • in London
  • at the airport
  • on holiday
  • in the evening
  • in 2018

We use prepositions:

  • in -> cities, years, parts of the day
  • at -> specific places (airport, hotel)
  • on -> holidays, days
