Future Continuous

Grammar Point #1 ‚Äî Future Continuous (Form)

Structure Examples
+ Subject + will be + verb-ing I will be working at midday.
She will be meeting the client this week.
- Subject + will not (won't) be + verb-ing I won't be working at 9am.
She won't be meeting the client next week.
? Will + subject + be + verb-ing? Will you be working at midday?
Will she be meeting the client?

Uses of Future Continuous

We use the Future Continuous to talk about:

  • Actions in progress at a specific time in the future ("This time tomorrow, I'll be flying to Paris.")
  • Planned or scheduled events ("I'll be presenting the report at 2pm.")
  • Polite questions about someone's plans ("Will you be using the meeting room this afternoon?")

Grammar Point #2 ‚Äî Future Continuous vs Present Continuous for the future

Use Example
Future Continuous ‚Äì used to describe an action in progress at a future time At 10am tomorrow, I'll be meeting the new client.
Present Continuous ‚Äì used for fixed plans or arrangements I'm meeting the new client at 10am tomorrow.

üí¨ Note: Both forms can refer to the future, but the Future Continuous focuses more on the activity in progress at that moment. The distinction can be subtle and native speakers sometimes use the two tenses interchangeably.

Grammar Point #3 ‚Äî Future Continuous for polite questions

We often use Future Continuous to ask about someone's plans in a polite and less direct way.

  • "Will you be using this desk later?"
  • "Will you be joining us for lunch?"

This sounds more polite than: "Will you use this desk later?" / "Will you join us for lunch?"
