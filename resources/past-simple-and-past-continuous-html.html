Past Simple & Past Continuous

Grammar Focus #1: Compare the Tenses

Past Simple

Structure:
• Regular verbs: verb + -ed (walked, played, studied)
• Irregular verbs: special forms (went, saw, ate)

Use:
• Completed actions in the past
• Actions that happened one after another
• Specific time mentioned (yesterday, last week, in 2020)

Examples:
I watched a film last night.
She arrived at 8 o'clock and started working immediately. 		Past Continuous

Structure:
was/were + verb-ing

Use:
• Actions in progress at a specific time in the past
• Background actions/setting the scene
• Interrupted actions (often with past simple)
• Two actions happening at the same time

Examples:
At 9pm, I was studying English.
While I was cooking, my sister was watching TV.

Past Simple + Past Continuous Together

When one action interrupts another:

  • I was walking to school when I saw my friend.
  • She was reading a book when the phone rang.

Pattern: Past Continuous (longer action) + WHEN/WHILE + Past Simple (shorter/interrupting action)

Speaking #1: Past Simple & Past Continuous

Discuss these questions:

  1. What were you doing at this time last week?
  2. What did you do when you woke up this morning? What did you do next?
  3. What were you doing when you heard some important news recently?
  4. What happened in the last film or series you watched?
  5. What happened in the last book you read?
  6. Think about your last birthday. How were you feeling when you woke up? What happened during the day?
  7. Tell me about a time when the weather suddenly changed. What were you doing?
  8. Describe an embarrassing moment. What were you doing when it happened?
  9. What were you wearing the last time you went to a special event? What happened there?
  10. Tell me about a time when you met someone new. What were you doing at the time?
