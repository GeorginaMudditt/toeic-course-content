Introduction to TOEICⓇ Writing

The Writing section within the TOEICⓇ 4-Skills Test has three different tasks, and lasts one hour:

  • Photos (5 photos) — Test takers see a selection of photos, each with two words or phrases to use in a sentence; they must write a sentence to describe each picture, using the two words/phrases given in each case.
  • Response (2 emails) — Test takers see email messages, with some information about what they should include in their response; they write a response to the emails.
  • Essay (1 topic) — Test takers are given a topic; they are then asked to give their opinion on the topic. The essay should be a minimum of 300 words.

Photos

Write a sentence based on a picture

Directions: In this part of the test, you will write ONE sentence that is based on a picture. With each picture you will be given TWO words or phrases that you must use in your sentence. You can change the forms of the words and you can use the words in any order.

Look at the example below. Then write your own sentence for each of the five photos, using both keywords given in each case.

Example

kitchen   |   prepare

You could write a simple sentence: “They are preparing dinner in the kitchen.”

Or you could write a more complex sentence for a higher score: “A man and a woman are busy preparing dinner in the kitchen, while another person is pouring water from a jug at the dining table.”

Response

Respond to a written request

Directions: In this part of the test, you will show how well you can write a response to an e-mail. Your response will be scored on the quality and variety of your sentences, vocabulary and organisation. You will have 10 minutes to read and answer each e-mail.

E-mail 1

Directions: Read the e-mail.

From: fkraus@kystone.co.uk

To: trandall@kystone.co.uk

Subject: Business Leadership conference?

Sent: January 12, 11:34 A.M.

Tim,

I was invited to attend this year's Business Leadership conference, and I'm not sure if I should go. I know you went to last year's conference, but I think you said that you wouldn't attend it again. Could you give me some details about why you didn't like the conference?

Francine

Directions: Respond to the e-mail as if you attended the conference last year. In your e-mail, give THREE reasons.

E-mail 2

Directions: Read the e-mail.

From: Dale City Welcome Committee

To: Dale City Residents

Subject: Welcome to your new home!

Sent: July 23, 4:32 P.M.

Welcome! We would like to be the first to welcome you to Dale City. We know that there are many things to do when you move, from finding your way around town to setting up your utilities. Please contact us if you need any help at all.

Directions: Respond to the e-mail. Respond as if you have recently moved to a new city. In your e-mail to the committee, make at least TWO requests for information.

Essay

Write an opinion essay

Directions: Read the question below. You have 30 minutes to plan, write, and revise your essay. Typically, an effective response will contain a minimum of 300 words.

Some people work for the same company for their entire career. What do you think are the advantages and disadvantages of staying at one company?

Give reasons and examples to support your opinion.

Sample Answers

E-mail 1

Francine,

I hope this e-mail finds you well. With regard to your question, last year's conference was great, but the logistics were bad. I first of all had problems with my hotel room. It took me ages to check in and get the keys to my room.

Furthermore, they could not find my name on the list on the first day of the conference, which was rather annoying, and the lunchbreak was far too long!

Hope this helps.

Tim

E-mail 2

Sir or Madam,

Thank you for your welcome e-mail. I have just moved to Dale City. To begin with, could you please give me some information on basketball clubs? Is there a list of clubs or sports centers you could give me?

I have another request: we are theatergoers. Where can we find information on theaters and concert halls?

Could you lastly please recommend a local bank?

Thank you very much in advance.

Yours faithfully,
Vanessa Alf

Checklist

Does my response include the following?

  • Did I respond to the instructions given in the prompt?
  • Did I use an appropriate level of formality (register)?
  • Did I organise my e-mail clearly (greeting, body, closing)?
  • Did I use a variety of sentences and vocabulary?
  • Did I use linking words to connect my ideas?
  • Did I check my spelling and grammar?

Essay

There are people who are perfectly content to work for the same company all their working life, even though this situation is becoming less common. In my opinion, there are both advantages and disadvantages to this type of career path.

One notable advantage of staying in the same company for all of your career is that you will avoid the stress that changing jobs and companies can create. Indeed, starting a new job is apparently one of the most stressful situations that we can experience as an adult. Furthermore, by staying at the same company, an employee demonstrates their loyalty to the firm. Similarly, they can feel as if they belong to a family.

On the contrary, there are a lot more disadvantages to staying in the same company for too long. For instance, people get used to seeing you in a particular way. This may make it difficult to apply for a different position, as your superiors may not be able to picture you in a different role. Secondly, when you move between companies, your salary rises significantly, whereas moving from one position to another within the same company tends to limit any raises to the bare minimum. Finally, people who have only worked in one company may have a limited business outlook due to their relative lack of experience. For this reason, it may be difficult for them to find innovative solutions or fresh new ideas.

To sum up, as far as I can tell, the disadvantages outweigh the advantages. Staying in the same company for all your career gives you a sort of stability, but this can also mean stagnating in the same role, with nothing new to stimulate you. In order to keep moving forward, it is probably better to change companies from time to time.
