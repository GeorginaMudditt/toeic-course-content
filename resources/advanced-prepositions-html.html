Advanced Prepositions

Why are prepositions so difficult?

Main reasons

  • One preposition can have several meanings
  • English uses many fixed expressions
  • The choice of preposition is not always logical
  • Some combinations depend on context and nuance

Professional English uses prepositions constantly in

  • Meetings
  • Presentations
  • Negotiations
  • Emails
  • Reports
  • Project management

Examples:

  • involved in a project
  • responsible for client communication
  • opposed to the proposal
  • recover from financial losses
  • contribute to company growth
💬 Good strategy: Instead of learning isolated prepositions, try to learn complete expressions.

Grammar Focus #1: Time prepositions: at / on / in

You already know the basic rules:

PrepositionTypical useExample
atprecise timeat 3pm
ondays & dateson Thursday
inmonths, years, periodsin Q4

Now let’s look at more professional and business-related expressions.

AT
  • at the moment
  • at short notice
  • at the end of the quarter
  • at the beginning of the meeting
ON
  • on schedule
  • on arrival
  • on business
  • on behalf of
IN
  • in advance
  • in the long term
  • in the meantime
  • in the early stages of the project
💬 Compare:

The consultant arrived on time for the client presentation.

We arrived in time to review the contract before the meeting.

💬 Difference: on time = punctual · in time = early enough

Grammar Focus #2: Place prepositions: in / at / on

IN

Use IN for: departments · offices · buildings · countries, cities and regions

Examples:

  • in the HR department
  • in the conference room
  • in Singapore
AT

Use AT for: specific points · events · work locations

Examples:

  • at reception
  • at the annual conference
  • at head office
ON

Use ON for: floors · surfaces · transport routes · business streets/avenues

Examples:

  • on the fifth floor
  • on the main desk
  • on Park Avenue

Grammar Focus #3: Dependent prepositions

Many adjectives, nouns and verbs are followed by particular prepositions.

Adjective + preposition

AdjectivePrepositionExample
responsibleforShe is responsible for staff training.
involvedinHe was involved in the negotiations.
capableofThey are capable of managing international accounts.
committedtoThe company is committed to sustainability.
familiarwithAre you familiar with the new software?

Verb + preposition

VerbPrepositionExample
specialiseinThe firm specialises in data security.
contributetoGood communication contributes to productivity.
relyonThe department relies on external consultants.
dealwithShe deals with customer complaints.
dependonThe schedule depends on supplier delivery times.
copewithMany managers struggle to cope with stress.
💬 There are usually no rules or logic for prepositions! Just try to learn the whole expression together.

Grammar Focus #4: Movement and direction

TO

Use TO for movement toward a destination.

  • travel to a conference
  • go to head office
INTO / OUT OF

Use INTO and OUT OF for movement in and out.

  • walk into the boardroom
  • leave through the emergency exit
THROUGH

Movement inside a space or process

  • move through the approval process
  • walk through the production facility
ACROSS

Movement from one side to the other

  • transfer staff across departments
  • travel across the city for meetings
ALONG

Use ALONG for movement beside a line.

  • walk along the corridor
  • drive along the industrial zone
AROUND

Use AROUND for movement or position within an area, or visiting several places in no fixed order.

  • walk around the office / factory floor
  • travel around the region meeting clients
  • stand around waiting for the meeting to start

Grammar Focus #5: Common confusing pairs

BY vs UNTIL

BY = before or no later than a deadline

Please submit the report by Friday.

UNTIL = continuing up to a point in time

The meeting continued until 8pm.

FOR vs SINCE

FOR = duration

for six months

SINCE = starting point

since January

DURING vs FOR

DURING = when something happens

during the presentation

FOR = duration of time

for three hours
