Introduction to TOEICⓇ Reading

The Reading section within the TOEICⓇ 4-Skills Test has three parts with a total of 45 questions, and lasts approximately 40 minutes:

  • Incomplete Sentences (12 questions) — Test takers read a phrase with a missing word; they choose which word completes the sentence from four options. This section tests grammar and vocabulary.
  • Text Completion (8 questions) — Test takers read longer texts with four blanks each; for each blank, they choose how to complete the sentence from four options. This section also tests grammar and vocabulary.
  • Reading Comprehension (25 questions) — Test takers read short texts and answer multiple-choice comprehension questions about each.

The Reading test is adaptive.

This means that the results obtained in the first part of the test determine the difficulty level of the questions in the second part of the test.

📝 Answer Key

Reading #1: Incomplete Sentences

  1. receipt — A receipt is proof that you paid for something; it is what you keep for a refund.
  2. had already presented — "By the time" + past simple ("arrived") needs past perfect for the earlier action: the team had already presented.
  3. expansion — After "rapid" we need a noun; "expansion" describes growth over time.
  4. update — "Update files" is the natural collocation for keeping records current.
  5. to have completed — The blank must complete “required …”: “required to have completed” means the training must already be finished by the deadline.
  6. recommend — The subject "We" needs a main verb; "recommend" fits "We strongly recommend that …".
  7. situated — Buildings are "situated" or "located" in a place; "situated near" is standard.
  8. will be distributed — The report receives the action (passive) and the time clause points to the future: will be distributed.
  9. compliant — After "are" we need an adjective: "compliant with" means in line with the law.
  10. reimburse — Organisers pay speakers back for costs: they reimburse travel expenses.
  11. were — With "neither … nor", the verb agrees with the nearer subject "colleagues" (plural): were.
  12. efficient — "Way" is a noun, so we need an adjective before it: an efficient way.
