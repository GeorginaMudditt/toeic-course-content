Introduction to TOEICⓇ Reading

The Reading section within the TOEICⓇ 4-Skills Test has three parts with a total of 45 questions, and lasts approximately 40 minutes:

  • Incomplete Sentences (12 questions) — Test takers read a phrase with a missing word; they choose which word completes the sentence from four options. This section tests grammar and vocabulary.
  • Text Completion (8 questions) — Test takers read longer texts with four blanks each; for each blank, they choose how to complete the sentence from four options. This section also tests grammar and vocabulary.
  • Reading Comprehension (25 questions) — Test takers read short texts and answer multiple-choice comprehension questions about each.

The Reading test is adaptive.

This means that the results obtained in the first part of the test determine the difficulty level of the questions in the second part of the test.

ARTICLE

Remote Work Becomes the New Normal for Many UK Firms

Six years after the widespread shift to home working during Covid, a growing number of UK companies are choosing to make remote or hybrid working arrangements permanent. A recent survey conducted by the Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development (CIPD) found that 58% of organisations now offer some form of flexible working as a standard condition of employment, up from just 30% five years ago.

Businesses cite a range of benefits, including reduced office overhead costs and improved employee satisfaction. However, the shift has also presented challenges. Managers frequently report difficulties in maintaining team cohesion, onboarding new employees effectively, and ensuring that junior staff receive adequate mentoring and professional development.

Technology has played a central role in enabling this transition. Platforms such as video conferencing tools and shared project management software have become standard features of the modern workplace. Many organisations have invested significantly in cybersecurity infrastructure to protect sensitive data shared across remote networks.

Not all industries have embraced the change equally. Sectors such as manufacturing, healthcare, and retail — where physical presence is essential — have seen much lower rates of adoption. By contrast, the finance, legal, and technology sectors have led the way, with some firms reducing their office space by as much as 40%.

Looking ahead, experts predict that fully remote roles will become increasingly common at senior levels, while junior and graduate positions are more likely to require regular in-person attendance. Companies that fail to develop clear, consistent policies on flexible working risk losing talent to competitors who offer greater autonomy.

📝 Answer Key

Reading #1: Incomplete Sentences

  1. C. receipt — A receipt is proof that you paid for something; it is what you keep for a refund.
  2. B. had already presented — "By the time" + past simple ("arrived") needs past perfect for the earlier action: the team had already presented.
  3. C. expansion — After "rapid" we need a noun; "expansion" describes growth over time.
  4. B. update — "Update files" is the natural collocation for keeping records current.
  5. B. to have completed — The blank must complete “required …”: “required to have completed” means the training must already be finished by the deadline.
  6. B. recommend — The subject "We" needs a main verb; "recommend" fits "We strongly recommend that …".
  7. B. situated — Buildings are "situated" or "located" in a place; "situated near" is standard.
  8. A. will be distributed — The report receives the action (passive) and the time clause points to the future: will be distributed.
  9. C. compliant — After "are" we need an adjective: "compliant with" means in line with the law.
  10. C. reimburse — Organisers pay speakers back for costs: they reimburse travel expenses.
  11. B. were — With "neither … nor", the verb agrees with the nearer subject "colleagues" (plural): were.
  12. C. efficient — "Way" is a noun, so we need an adjective before it: an efficient way.

Reading #2: Text Completion

  1. C. To begin with — This phrase introduces the first step in a sequence of actions about the move.
  2. C. loss — The fixed phrase is "data loss" (a noun), not "data lose/losing/lost".
  3. D. is attached — A floor plan is sent with the email; passive present "is attached to this email" is correct here.
  4. B. Should you have any questions, please do not hesitate to contact me directly. — This closing sentence fits naturally before "Best regards" and matches the helpful tone of the message.
  5. A. announce — Organisations are "pleased to announce" a new programme; the other verbs do not collocate with "pleased to" here.
  6. B. accommodate — "Accommodate growing demand" means to make room for more people who want to join.
  7. B. Additionally — The website is a second way to get forms, so we need an additive linker (not "However" or "Despite").
  8. A. receive — After "will" we need the base verb: participants will receive a discount.
