Grammar Point #2 ‚Äì Past Continuous with "when" and "while"

We often use the Past Continuous with the Past Simple to show that one action was in progress when another action interrupted it.

Structure:

  • Past Continuous + when + Past Simple
  • While + Past Continuous, Past Simple

Examples:

  • I was cooking when the phone rang.
  • While I was cooking, the phone rang.

Note: These two examples mean exactly the same thing; they show two possible structures to express the same meaning.
