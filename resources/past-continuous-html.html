Past Continuous

Grammar reference and practice

Important note:

En français, on utilise l'imparfait pour décrire une action qui était en train de se passer dans le passé (Je travaillais quand tu as appelé). En anglais, on utilise le Past Continuous pour exprimer exactement la même idée.

En anglais :

Past Simple = action complétée dans le passé (J'ai travaillé hier → I worked yesterday)

Past Continuous = action en cours dans le passé (Je travaillais à 8h → I was working at 8 a.m.) ou action interrompue par une autre action

Grammar Point #1: Past Continuous

+ - ?
Structure Subject + was/were + verb-ing Subject + was/were + not + verb-ing Was/Were + subject + verb-ing?
Examples I was working.
He was watching television.
We were travelling.		 I was not concentrating.
She was not listening.
They were not eating.		 Was I speaking clearly?
Was she leaving?
Were they driving?
Notes

We often use the contracted form:

I wasn't concentrating.

She wasn't listening.

They weren't eating.

These questions are often answered with short answers:

Yes, I was. / No, I wasn't.

Yes, she was. / No, she wasn't.

Yes, they were. / No, they weren't.

Grammar Point #2: Past Continuous with "when" and "while"

We often use the Past Continuous with the Past Simple to show that one action was in progress when another action interrupted it.

Structure:

  • Past Continuous + when + Past Simple
  • When/While + Past Continuous, Past Simple

Examples:

  • I was cooking when the phone rang.
  • When/While I was cooking, the phone rang.

Note: These two examples mean exactly the same thing; they show two possible structures to express the same meaning.

Important note:

Comme avec le Present Continuous, certains verbes (state verbs / stative verbs) ne sont normalement pas utilisés avec le Past Continuous. Ces verbes décrivent un état (situation, sentiment, opinion, possession…) et non une action.

Exemples corrects :

  • I liked my job.
  • She wanted a coffee.
  • I needed help.
  • I understood the question.

Formes incorrectes :

  • ❌ I was liking my job.
  • ❌ She was wanting a coffee.
  • ❌ I was needing help.
  • ❌ I was understanding the question.
