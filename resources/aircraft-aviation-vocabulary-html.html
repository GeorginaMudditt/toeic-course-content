Practice #1: Parts of an aircraft

  1. The aircraft accelerated along the runway and lifted into the air.
  2. After landing, the plane was parked inside the hangar.
  3. Mechanics checked the engine before take-off.
  4. The wing on each side helps the plane stay in the air.
  5. Passengers and luggage are carried inside the fuselage.
  6. A small plane has a propeller at the front that pushes it forward.
  7. The pilot lowered the landing gear just before the plane touched down.
  8. The pilot sits in the cockpit and controls the aircraft.
  9. Each rotor blade on the helicopter spins very fast.
  10. The tail at the rear keeps the aircraft stable during flight.

Practice #2: Aviation people & cockpit instruments

  1. The altimeter shows how high the aircraft is flying.
  2. The ground crew refuelled the aircraft after it landed.
  3. The pilot radioed the tower before take-off.
  4. All flights at the airport are coordinated from the control tower.
  5. Each paratrooper jumped from the open door of the transport plane.
  6. The navigator planned the safest route across the mountains.
  7. Push the throttle forward to increase speed.
  8. The air traffic controller told the pilot to wait on the runway.
  9. The co-pilot monitored the instruments during the long flight.
  10. The pilot moved the control stick / yoke to turn the aircraft left.

Vocabulary #3: Aviation adjectives — opposites

  • Smooth — Turbulent
  • Heavy — Light
  • Clear — Cloudy
  • Airborne — Grounded
  • High — Low
  • Steady — Unstable
  • Fast — Slow
  • Manned — Unmanned
