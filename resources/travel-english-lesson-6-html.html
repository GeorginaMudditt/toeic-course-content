Reading: The Car Rental Problem

Melissa and Ravi arrived at Barcelona airport at 10 p.m. on a Monday in August. They were very tired after their long flight from Sydney. They wanted to rent a car for their holiday.

They walked to the car rental desk and showed their reservation confirmation to the man at the desk. The man looked at his computer for a long time. Then he looked worried. He could not find their reservation in the system. Melissa and Ravi were very surprised because they booked the car two months ago.

The man checked his computer again but there was no reservation. He offered them other cars. First, he offered them a manual car, but Melissa and Ravi could only drive automatic cars. Then the man found one automatic car. It was a BMW and it looked very nice. But there was a problem: it cost 150 euros per day. This was too expensive for them. Their budget was only 40 euros per day.

Melissa and Ravi thought about their options. It was late and they were very tired. They left the car rental desk and took a taxi to their hotel in the city centre. The next day at the hotel, they made a new plan. Barcelona had excellent public transport, so they did not really need a car.

They bought metro tickets. For the rest of their holiday, they travelled around the city by metro, bus, and tram. They saved a lot of money and they enjoyed seeing the city like the local people. In the end, they were happy that they didn’t have a car because the traffic in Barcelona was terrible!

Questions

  1. What time did Melissa and Ravi arrive at Barcelona airport?
  2. What was the problem at the car rental desk?
  3. Why didn’t they take the manual car?
  4. How much did the BMW cost per day?
  5. How did they get to their hotel?
  6. What did they use for transport during their holiday?
  7. Were they pleased with their decision? Why?
