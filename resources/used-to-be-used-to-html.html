Used to & Be used to

Introduction

  • Think about when you were younger: what things did you do that you don't do anymore?
  • And what new things have you started doing recently that felt strange at first?

Grammar Focus #1: USED TO (past habits and states)

+ - ?
Structure:
subject + used to + base verb

Examples:
I used to play tennis twice a week.
She used to live in Paris. 		Structure:
subject + didn't use to + base verb

Examples:
I didn't use to like coffee, but now I love it.
They didn't use to work together. 		Structure:
Did + subject + use to + base verb ?

Examples:
Did you use to watch cartoons after school?
Did they use to have a red car?

  2. What kind of books did you use to like when you first learned to read?
  3. Where did you use to go on holiday when you were a child?

Grammar Focus #2: BE USED TO (familiarity)

+ - ?
Structure:
subject + be + used to + noun / pronoun / -ing verb

Examples:
I am used to getting up early.
He was used to living alone. 		Structure:
subject + be + not + used to + noun / pronoun / -ing verb

Examples:
She is not used to the cold weather.
They were not used to the Australian accent. 		Structure:
Are/Is + subject + used to + noun / pronoun / -ing verb ?

Examples:
Are you used to driving on the left now?
Is he used to working night shifts?

  3. French students ................ wearing school uniforms.
  4. ................ (your son) his new teacher?
  5. He does like his new car, but he ................ (not/be) driving it yet.

Speaking #2: Be used to

Discuss these questions:

  1. Are you used to spicy food? Why/Why not?
  2. Is your mum used to helping you with your homework?
  3. What aspects of English culture are you used to now? What are you still not used to?

Practice #3: Sentence Completion (Used to & Be used to)

Complete the sentences using "used to" or "be used to" in the correct form:

  1. When I was younger, I ................ (play) football every day, but now I ................ (not/be) so active.
  2. Sarah ................ (live) in London, so she ................ the public transport system there.
  3. I ................ (not/be) spicy food when I first moved to India, but now I ................ (be) it.
  4. You seem to have three cups of coffee every morning, so you must ................ (be) the caffeine!
  5. My grandmother ................ (write) letters by hand, but now she types letters and prints them. She ................ (not/be) using email yet - it's still difficult for her.
  6. We ................ (go) camping every summer when my children were young. Now my children are grown up and they ................ (not/be) sleeping outdoors because they always stay in hotels.
  7. Tom ................ (be) a vegetarian, but he gave it up last year. Now he ................ (be) eating meat again.
  8. ................ you ................ (be) working from home now, or do you still find it strange? I know you ................ (work) in an office before the pandemic.
  9. I ................ (not/be) such cold winters when I first arrived in Canada, but after five years I ................ (be) the snow.
  10. My parents ................ (travel) a lot for work, so they ................ (be) long flights and different time zones.
  11. She ................ (think) English grammar was impossible, but now she ................ (be) studying it every day and it feels much easier.
  12. ................ you ................ (walk) to school when you were little? My son has just started walking and he ................ (not/be) it yet - he still asks me to drive him!
