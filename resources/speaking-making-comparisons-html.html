Startups tend to offer a more dynamicer and less hierarchical environment than large corporations, but they also carry significantly more risk. In a big company, the structures are more clearer, the benefits are more generous, and job security is far greater. However, progression can be slower and the culture is often more rigid. In a startup, you’re likely to take on a wider range of responsibility much earlier in your career, which can be extremely valuable. Job security vs job satisfaction

This is one of the most debated questions in modern working life. Job security is undoubtedly more important during periods of economic uncertainty, when financial pressure makes stability the priority. However, numerous studies suggest that job satisfy has a far greater impact on long-term wellbeing and productivity. The least motivated employees are often those who feel trapped in a secure but unfulfilling role. Ideally, the most good situation is one where both aspects are present, but if forced to choose, most people under fifty tend to prioritise satisfaction, while those closer to retirement often place greater value at security.
