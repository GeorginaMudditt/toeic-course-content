TOEICⓇ Speaking: Describing an Image

Speaking

The TOEIC 4-Skills Speaking test includes a task called "Photographs". In this task, the test taker describes two images they see on the screen, one by one.

These are the instructions you will see on the screen:

DESCRIBE A PICTURE

Directions: In this part of the test, you will describe the picture on your screen in as much detail as you can. You will have 45 seconds to prepare your response. Then you will have 30 seconds to speak about the picture.

Strategies for success

  1. Look at the image globally and identify the context. Where is the photo taken? What is happening?
    e.g. "A woman is pulling a suitcase towards a bus shelter."
  2. Think about useful vocabulary to describe:
    • The people and objects visible
      e.g. "woman/women, bus shelter, suitcase, handbag, phone, bench, map"
    • Their location
      e.g. "city/town, street/road, signs, parked cars, bus, shops/flats"
    • Their actions
      e.g. "walking, pulling, talking/chatting, sitting, looking for"
      !! You will use mostly the Present Continuous tense here, to describe something happening at the moment !!
  3. Start your description, firstly giving a general description.
    e.g. "The place in the photo looks like a bus shelter in a city. We can see a woman walking towards it, who is pulling a suitcase."
  4. Now time to "zoom in" on the details and describe fully what you can see in the photograph.
    e.g. "We can also see two women sitting on the bench under the bus shelter. The one on the left is talking on her mobile phone. The one on the right is rummaging through her handbag, as if she is looking for something. The bus that the women are waiting for can be seen arriving in the distance. On the left of the photograph, on the road, we can see some cars and some road signs. On the right, behind the bus shelter, there are some buildings - probably shops or flats."
