Gerunds and Infinitives

Grammar Point #1 ‚Äî Two verbs together

In English, a verb is often followed by another verb.

The second verb usually changes into one of these forms:

  • Gerund (e.g. running)
  • Infinitive with "to" (e.g. to run)

üëâ The form depends on the first verb.

Definition: A gerund is a word ending in "-ing" that is made from a verb and used like a noun.

Examples

I enjoy working with my team.

She wants to improve her English.

Grammar Point #2 ‚Äî Verbs with both forms (where the meaning changes)

Some verbs can be followed by both a Gerund or an Infinitive. If the verbs below are followed by a Gerund, the meaning is different from if they are followed by an Infinitive.

Verb Gerund Infinitive
stop finish doing something stop in order to do something
remember remember a past action remember to do a future action
try experiment make an effort

Practice #2 ‚Äî Choose the correct form

  1. I stopped (drinking / to drink) coffee last year after I had problems sleeping.
  2. I stopped (to buy / buying) coffee on my way to work because I didn't have time to have breakfast at home.
  3. Remember (to send / sending) the email to the suppliers before the end of the day.
  4. I remember (to meet / meeting) him at the Prague conference in 2018.
  5. Try (to restart / restarting) the computer.
  6. You should try (to do / doing) a bit more exercise, even 15 minutes a day.

Grammar Point #3 ‚Äî Preposition + Gerund

After a preposition, use a gerund.

Examples

I'm interested in learning English.

She is good at managing people.

They talked about moving premises.

Grammar Point #4 ‚Äî Object + Infinitive

After an object, use an infinitive.

Examples

I asked her to send the report.

They told us to finish the work.

She reminded him to call the client.
