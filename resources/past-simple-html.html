Complete the grammar rules for present simple > past simple

  1. Most regular verbs add (talk > talked, work > worked)
  2. Verbs ending in "e" add (love > loved, arrive > arrived)
  3. Verbs ending in consonant + y, change "y" to (study > studied)
  4. Verbs ending in consonant, vowel, consonant often the final consonant (stop > stopped, plan > planned)

Reading #1: Past Simple

A Sailing Holiday

Last summer, Tom and Eddie went on a sailing holiday in Greece. They rented a small boat for one week. The weather was beautiful and sunny every day.

They sailed to quiet beaches. They swam in the clear blue water and relaxed on the sand. Eddie collected some pretty shells on the beach.

They visited a small fishing village. They walked around the harbour and talked to some friendly local people. They ate fresh fish at a restaurant by the sea. It was delicious!

They explored a tiny island. They climbed to the top of a hill and enjoyed the amazing view. Tom took lots of photos.

The wind stopped for a few days, so they stayed near one beach. They read books and listened to music. In the evening, they watched the sunset together.

On the last day, they sailed back to the port. They returned the boat and thanked the owner.

Tom and Eddie loved their sailing adventure. They want to come back next year.

Grammar rules completion:

  1. Most regular verbs add -ed (talk > talked, work > worked)
  2. Verbs ending in "e" add -d (love > loved, arrive > arrived)
  3. Verbs ending in consonant + y, change "y" to "ied" (study > studied)
  4. Verbs ending in consonant, vowel, consonant often double the final consonant (stop > stopped, plan > planned)
