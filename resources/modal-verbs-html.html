What is a modal verb?

‚úèÔ∏è Definition:

A modal verb is a special verb that we use with another verb to show the speaker's attitude ‚Äì for example, to talk about possibility, ability, obligation, advice, or permission.

‚úì Key rules:
  • Modal verbs do not change (no '-s' in the third person).
  • Modal verbs are followed by the base form of the main verb (infinitive without to). The only exception is 'have to'.
    ‚úó She can to swim ‚Üí ‚úì She can swim.
  • We can use modal verbs to make our language more precise or polite.
‚Üí Common modal verbs:

can, could, may, might, must, shall, should, will, would

Modals help you sound more natural and express differences in meaning, not just facts.

Grammar Focus #2

The main function of modal verbs

Function Modal verbs Example
Deduction / Certainty must, might, can't "That must be her." "She might be stuck."
Suggestion should, could "You should take the bus."
Ability & Possibility can, could, may, might "I can swim." "He may help."
Obligation & Necessity must, should, have to "You must finish your work."
Permission & Requests can, could, may "Could I speak to Henry, please?"
Regret & Unreal Situations should have, could have "I should have studied more."
Ideal situations should, would "In a perfect world, people would live in harmony."

Practice #1 : Choose the correct option

  1. She worked for 12 hours yesterday so she ............ be tired. (must, might, can't)
  2. I ............ speak German because I lived there for several years, but I ............ speak Italian. (must, can, can't)
  3. He ............ play that song on the guitar when he was 8 years old. (can, can't, could)
  4. My doctor said that I ............ give up smoking. (might, must, may)
  5. ............ I open this window, please? (should, must, could)
  6. You ............ wear a helmet when you rent a bike from here. (have to, might, can't)
  7. She ............ study harder if she wants to pass her exams. (can, has to, may)
  8. You ............ drive too fast on the roads around here, especially at night. (don't have to, shouldn't, can't)
  9. If you enjoy travelling, perhaps you ............ take a gap year. (have to, must, should)
  10. I ............ got up earlier this morning - I have so much to do. (should have, must have, have to)

Speaking #1 : Rules

What rules are there at your workplace? How do these rules compare to when you were at school?

Use modal verbs to talk about the rules - present and past.

üìù Answers

Practice #1 : Choose the correct option

  1. must - She worked for 12 hours, so it's very likely she's tired (deduction/certainty)
  2. can / can't - I have the ability to speak German, but not Italian
  3. could - Past ability (when he was 8 years old)
  4. must - Strong obligation/necessity (doctor's advice)
  5. Could - Polite request for permission
  6. have to - Obligation/necessity (rule when renting)
  7. has to - Obligation/necessity (required to pass)
  8. shouldn't - Advice/strong recommendation (negative)
  9. should - Suggestion/advice
  10. should have - Regret about past action (past modal)
