Disagreement and Criticism

Listening · C1

Speaking

Discuss the following questions.

  • “If you don’t have anything nice to say, don’t say anything at all.” How far do you agree with this statement?
  • How comfortable are you disagreeing with people at work?
  • Is avoiding conflict usually a good idea in a professional setting? Why / Why not?
  • What’s the difference between criticism and constructive criticism?
  • Have you ever received criticism at work that was particularly helpful? What made it effective?
  • Have you ever received criticism at work that upset or offended you?
  • Can being too polite sometimes create misunderstandings?
  • Is it easier to disagree with a colleague, a manager or a customer? Why?
  • How direct do you think people should be when giving negative feedback?
  • In French culture, do you think disagreement and criticism are generally expressed directly or indirectly? How does this compare to other cultures you know?

Vocabulary

A. Disagreement

Soft disagreement

  • I’m not entirely convinced by that.
  • I see your point, but…
  • That’s a fair point. However…
  • I can understand where you’re coming from, although…
  • I’m not sure that’s the best approach.
  • I’d look at it slightly differently.
  • I’m afraid I don’t quite agree.
  • We may have to agree to disagree.

Firmer disagreement

  • I don’t think that’s the right solution.
  • I have some reservations about that.
  • I see a few issues with that proposal.
  • I’m concerned that this approach could…
  • I’d strongly question whether…
  • I respectfully disagree.

B. Constructive Criticism

Introducing criticism politely

  • Could I make one suggestion?
  • May I offer some feedback?
  • If I could make one observation…
  • One thing that might improve this is…
  • Have you considered…?

Giving criticism

  • The presentation was clear, but it could be more concise.
  • I think the report would benefit from more data.
  • The design doesn’t fully meet the client’s brief.
  • There are a few areas that could be strengthened.
  • I’m concerned that customers might find this confusing.
  • I think we need to rethink this section.

Ending positively

  • Overall, it’s a strong piece of work.
  • It’s definitely moving in the right direction.
  • Once we’ve addressed those points, I think it’ll be much stronger.
  • I really like the overall direction.

Audioscript

Emma: Thanks for joining, everyone. I’d like us to finalise the plans for the product launch today. My proposal is to hold the event on 15 September.

Daniel: I’m not entirely convinced that’s the best option.

Emma: Really? What’s your concern?

Daniel: Well, I see your point—the earlier we launch, the sooner we can generate publicity. However, the sales team won’t have completed their training by then, and I’m worried customers will have questions we simply won’t be able to answer.

Emma: That’s a fair point, but delaying the launch could mean missing an important opportunity.

Ishani: If I could make one suggestion, perhaps we’re looking at this as an either-or decision. Have we considered a smaller launch in September, followed by a larger event a few weeks later?

Emma: That’s an interesting idea.

Daniel: It certainly has potential, although I’m concerned it might increase our costs.

Ishani: Actually, I think we’d be better off spending less on the initial event. Last year we invited nearly two hundred people, and, if I’m honest, it didn’t really add much value.

Emma: Are you suggesting we reduce the guest list?

Ishani: Yes. I think a more focused event would create a better experience for the people who attend.

Daniel: I agree with that. One thing I would question, though, is the proposed venue. It’s impressive, but I think it’s bigger than we need.

Emma: We chose it because it reflects the company’s image.

Daniel: I understand the reasoning, but I don’t think customers decide to buy our products because the venue looks expensive.

Emma: Fair enough.

Ishani: Could I offer one piece of feedback about last year’s launch?

Emma: Of course.

Ishani: Overall, it was successful, but there were too many last-minute changes. Speakers received updated schedules several times, and some guests weren’t sure where they were supposed to be. I think we need a much clearer plan this year.

Emma: I completely agree. We definitely don’t want to repeat those mistakes.

Daniel: Exactly. I’d rather have a smaller event that’s well organised than a large event that feels chaotic.

Emma: So, shall we say this: we hold a smaller launch in September, reduce the guest list, reconsider the venue, and organise a larger customer event later in the autumn?

Daniel: That sounds like a sensible compromise.

Ishani: I agree. Overall, I think we’re heading in the right direction.
