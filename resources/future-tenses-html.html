Future: will and going to

Grammar Point #1 ‚Äì Future with will

Structure Examples
+ Subject + will + base verb - I will send the email.
- She will call the client.
- Subject + will not (won't) + base verb - I won't send the email.
- She won't call the client.
? Will + subject + base verb? - Will you send the email?
- Will she call the client?

Uses of will:

  • decisions made at the moment of speaking ("The printer isn't working. I'll call IT.")
  • offers and promises ("I'll help you with the presentation.")
  • predictions based on opinion ("I think the meeting will be short.")

Grammar Point #2 ‚Äì Future with going to

Structure Examples
+ Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb - I am going to prepare the agenda.
- He is going to book the tickets.
- They are going to clean the factory.
- Subject + am/is/are not + going to + base verb - I am not going to prepare the agenda.
- He is not going to book the tickets.
- They are not going to clean the factory.
? Am/Is/Are + subject + going to + base verb? - Am I going to prepare the agenda?
- Is he going to book the tickets?
- Are they going to clean the factory?

Uses of going to

  • plans or intentions already decided ("We're going to hire a new assistant.")
  • future actions based on present evidence ("Look at that black cloud. It's going to rain.")

Grammar Point #3 ‚Äì Choosing between will and going to

Use Example
will
Decision made now It's hot in here. I'll open the window.
Offer / promise I'll get these documents back to you by tomorrow.
Opinion / guess I think social media will become less popular in the next ten years.
going to
Plan already decided I'm going to fly to Rio via Lisbon.
Intention I'm going to invite the clients for dinner.
Visible evidence Look how many people are here! It's going to be a busy day.
