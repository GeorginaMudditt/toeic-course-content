Part 3: Discussion Questions

Use the vocabulary from this worksheet to answer the following questions.

  1. What are the advantages and risks of keeping most of your money in a current account rather than in savings?
  2. What problems can people face when the value of their country's currency falls?
  3. When is it a good idea to borrow money, and when is it better to save and pay later?
  4. What is the difference between a broker, a trader, and an investor? Which role interests you most, and why?
  5. How do rising interest rates influence household borrowing, business investment, and the stock market?
  6. What signs might suggest that an economy is heading towards recession?

Part 2: Gap Fill Exercise

  1. Banks usually require the consent of all parties before closing a joint account, since either holder could otherwise be left without access to shared funds.
  2. A sudden fall on the stock market can wipe out a large part of the value of pension funds in a single day.
  3. Unable to raise enough capital from shareholders, the founders had to borrow from a specialist lender.
  4. The factory blamed rising commodities prices, especially for oil and steel, for its declining profit margins.
  5. Before approving the takeover, the board asked for an independent financial review of the target company's accounts.
  6. After three quarters of falling output and rising unemployment, the country was widely described as being in a recession.
  7. The bank allowed a temporary overdraft so the business could pay suppliers while waiting for customer invoices.
  8. A patient investor may accept lower short-term returns in exchange for steady long-term growth.
  9. If you only pay the minimum amount on your credit card each month, your debt can grow quickly because of interest charges.
  10. The agreement was expected to boost cross-border trade in services as well as manufactured goods.
  11. For day-to-day spending, she kept her money in a current account rather than in fixed-term deposits or a shared household account.
  12. She shopped around carefully because even a small difference in the interest rate could save thousands over the life of a mortgage.
  13. The insurance broker compared policies from several providers and recommended the best-value option.
  14. Slower economic growth forced the government to revise its public spending plans for the coming year.
  15. Rather than investing the money, they decided to build up their savings for unforeseen expenses.
  16. Exporters benefited when the national currency weakened, because their goods became cheaper for foreign buyers.
  17. To buy the van, she took out a small business loan and agreed to repay it in monthly instalments.
  18. The app alerts customers when their account balance falls below £100.
  19. On the exchange floor, a trader may buy and sell the same commodity several times in one session.
  20. For the fifth year running, government spending exceeded tax revenue, leaving a significant budget deficit.
