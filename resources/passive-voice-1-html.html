Passive Voice 1

Introduction

The passive voice is used when we want to focus on the action or the person/thing affected by the action, rather than who or what performs the action. Think about news reports: "The bank was robbed yesterday" focuses on the bank, not the robbers. The passive is often used when we don't know who did the action.

Active: Someone cleans the office every day.
Passive: The office is cleaned every day.

Grammar Focus: Passive Voice Forms

Tense Active Passive Structure (passive)
Present Simple They make cars here. Cars are made here. Subject + verb 'to be' (present) + past participle
Present Continuous They are building a new bridge. A new bridge is being built. Subject + verb 'to be' (present) + being + past participle
Past Simple Someone stole my bike. My bike was stolen. Subject + verb 'to be' (past) + past participle
Past Continuous They were repairing the road. The road was being repaired. Subject + verb 'to be' (past) + being + past participle
Future (will) They will open a new shop. A new shop will be opened. Subject + will be + past participle
Future (going to) They are going to change the rules. The rules are going to be changed. Subject + verb 'to be' (present) + going to be + past participle

Note: We use "by" to mention who does the action, but we often leave it out if it's not important or obvious (e.g. "My bike was stolen by thieves" or simply "My bike was stolen")

  5. The company will announce the winners next Friday.

    My laptop .................................... when I came home.

  • What is made or produced there
  • What buildings or facilities are being built or renovated

  • What changes were made in the past
Example: My town is known for its chocolate factory. The old cinema was demolished last year, and a new sports centre is being built in its place...
