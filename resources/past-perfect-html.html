Past Perfect (Simple & Continuous)

Grammar reference and practice

Grammar Focus #1: Compare the Tenses

Past Perfect Simple Past Perfect Continuous
Formation:
had + past participle
(had + verb-ed / had + irregular form)

Usage:
• Actions completed before another past action
• Experiences before a specific time in the past
• With "by the time", "before", "after", "already", "never"

Examples:
When I arrived, the meeting had already started.
She had finished her homework before dinner. 		Formation:
had + been + verb-ing

Usage:
• Actions in progress up to a point in the past
• Duration of an action before another past action
• With "for", "since", "how long"

Examples:
She had been working there for five years before she quit.
How long had you been waiting when the doctor arrived?
