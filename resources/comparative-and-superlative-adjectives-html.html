Grammar Focus #2: Comparison Structures

Basic comparison:
A is [comparative] than B
"My phone is newer than yours." 		As...as (equality):
A is as [adjective] as B
"My phone is as expensive as yours." 		Not as...as (inferiority):
A is not as [adjective] as B
"My phone is not as modern as yours."
Much/far/a lot (emphasising difference):
A is much/far/a lot [comparative] than B
"London is much/far bigger than Oxford." 		A little/slightly/a bit (small difference):
A is a little/slightly/a bit [comparative] than B
"I'm a little taller than my sister."

Speaking #1: Comparatives and Superlatives

  1. Do you think social media has made people more connected or more lonely than previous generations? Why?
